The GCCI offers a variety range of services to its members
The GCCI engages in advocacy efforts to represent and promote the interests of its members at various levels, including engaging with government agencies and advocating for policies and regulations that benefit the business community. They work to ensure a conducive busine
The GCCI facilitates business-to-business (B2B) networking opportunities for its members. This involves creating platforms and events where member businesses can connect, collaborate, and explore potential partnerships for mutual benefit.
The GCCI organizes business forums and discussions, providing a space for members to share insights, discuss industry trends, and address common challenges. These forums encourage knowledge sharing and collaboration among businesses.
GCCI organizes trade fairs, exhibitions, and expos where member businesses can showcase their products and services to a wider audience. These events provide opportunities to attract customers, investors, and partners.
The GCCI offers training programs and workshops to enhance the skills and knowledge of its members. These training initiatives can cover various aspects of business operations, compliance, market trends, and other relevant topics to improve the capabilities of member businesses.
GCCI provides advisory services to members, offering guidance and expertise on various business-related matters. This can include legal and regulatory advice, market entry strategies, business planning, and other consultancy services to support the growth and success of member businesses.
The GCCI organizes and supports trade missions, where members have the opportunity to explore international markets, establish business contacts, and engage with potential clients or partners in other countries. These missions aim to promote international trade and business expansion.
GCCI issues certificates of origin to members exporting their products internationally. A certificate of origin is an important document that verifies the origin of goods and can be required by importers to comply with trade agreements or customs regulations.
