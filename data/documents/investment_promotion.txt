Key Functions
Offering investor–facilitation services, the Agency acts as investors’ first point of contact, provides information on relevant procedures for setting up a business and helps form the necessary network of contacts in The Gambia for successful business operations.
GIEPA advises and collaborates with the Government of The Gambia, the private sector and civil society towards developing a globally competitive business environment. Key functions center on promoting the exportation of Gambian goods, developing and regulating The Gambia’s business park, formulating investment promotion, export and enterprise development strategies and policies geared towards the continuous improvement of the investment climate in The Gambia for private sector led growth.
Why Invest in The Gambia?
Business Friendly Environment
The Government of The Gambia is continually upgrading business procedures to transform The Gambia into a preferred location for profitable investments. Reforms to liberalize the ICT sector and the energy sector have already been completed. A variety of incentives for investors, including GIEPA's Special Investment Certificate (SIC) are also available.
Strategic Location & Access to West Africa
Driven by its geographical proximity to West African countries, The Gambia offers an excellent opportunity for investors to tap into a growing market with over 300m+ potential consumers. The Gambia also provides a port with access to shipping from the Atlantic ocean and a variety of preferential trade partners.
Liberal Market-Based Economy
Investors profit from a liberal, market-based economy characterized by sustained scale and monetary discipline, flexible exchange rate, and reliable laws.
The Gambia also ranked 41 out of 138 in the Labour Market Efficiency Index provided by the world Economic Forum in 2016-2017.
Source
Competitive Labor Costs
For decades, businesses have benefited from The Gambia’s abundant semi-skilled and unskilled labor force. Wages in The Gambia are substantially lower than in many West African countries across a variety of labor categories. At present, the minimum daily wage rate is $1.50 with average wages between $2.50 and $4 per day. This large pool of relatively cost-effective labor has helped businesses lower operational costs.
Peaceful and Politically-Stable Country
In 2018, The Gambia had the largest positive change in the Global Peace Index, moving up 35 places to rank 76th globally.
Source

Reasons to Consider Investing in The Gambia
Variety of Sector Opportunities
The Gambia is encouraging of investment across all sectors. Target sectors for investment include Agribusiness, ICT, Renewable Energy, Light Manufacturing, Transport and Tourism, but investment in other sectors is also welcome and may be eligible for incentives.
Peaceful and Politically-Stable Country
The Gambia is one of the safest countries in Africa. Called the “melting pot” of West Africa, The Gambia has a variety of ethnic groups that live peacefully with one another.
Competitive Labor Costs
For many decades, businesses have benefited from The Gambia’s abundant semi-skilled and unskilled labor force. Many wages in The Gambia are substantially lower than in many West African countries. At present, the minimum daily wage rate starts with $1.50 for unskilled labor but the average wages hover between $2.50 and $4 a day. This large pool of relatively cost-effective labor has helped many businesses lower operational costs.
Liberal Market-Based Economy
The Gambia maintains a liberal market economy characterised by sustained growth, monetary discipline, a flexible exchange rate system, and robust legal frameworks. These elements provide investors with a predictable and business-friendly environment.
Economic Freedom
: In the
2023 Index of Economic Freedom
, The Gambia achieved a score of
57.9
, ranking
101st
globally and
11th
among 47 countries in the Sub-Saharan Africa region.
Availability of Investment Incentives
Investors can receive a variety of investment incentives. GIEPA offers a Special Investment Certificate (SIC) which includes corporate tax relief, import duty exemption, VAT exemption and more.
Direct Market Access to West Africa
Driven by its geographical proximity to West African countries, The Gambia offers an excellent opportunity for investors to tap into a growing market with over 300m+ potential consumers.
Variety of Trade Partners
The Gambia benefits from a variety of trade agreements and preferential market access through its membership to the AfCFTA, ECOWAS, OIC and more.
Strategic Port Location
The Gambia is strategically positioned for trade and export with its location and port giving access to the Atlantic Ocean.
English-Speaking Country
English is the country’s official language and the most commonly spoken language in business arena.
Business Friendly Environment
The Government of The Gambia is continually upgrading business procedures to ensure an ideal business environment for investors. Key initiatives already completed include:
The SWBR Act of 2013
- This act introduced a new registration agency under the Ministry of Justice along with a new system to enable online registration and storage of documents. Under the new system, the online Single Window Business Registry (SWBR) is the single point of contact for starting a business, tax and social security registrations together.
Liberalization of the Energy Sector
- In 2012, The Gambia made accessing electricity faster and easier by allowing customers to choose private contractors to carry out external connection projects. In 2017, an energy sector roadmap was approved by the cabinet to modernize the energy sector and move towards ensuring 24/7 energy access to all Gambians.
Reforms to Corporate Tax Regime
- In 2012, The Gambia made paying taxes easier for companies by successfully replacing the sales tax with VAT. Then, in 2018, it made paying taxes less costly by reducing the corporate income tax rate from 30% to 27%. The rate on turnover was also reduced from 1.5% to 1% for audited accounts and from 2.5% to 2% for audited accounts.
ICT Investments to Improve Trade Environment -
Since 2009, The Gambia has improved its port infrastructure by investing in the rehabilitation of container terminals. The Gambia Revenue Authority (GRA) has also made trading across borders faster by investing in an ICT processing system to improve the efficiency of trade facilitation. There are also future plans to migrate to the ASYCUDA World system for management of the port. This is a web-based customs management system which will allow traders submit trade documents such as customs declarations and transit documents electronically.

GiEPA & PARTNERS STAGE THE GAMBIA - SENEGAL ECONOMIC, TRADE  AND INVESTMENT FORUM
26 Oct 2023
News
The Gambia Investment and Export Promotion Agency (GiEPA) in collaboration with the Agence Nationale Pour la Promotion des Investissements et des Grands Travaux - (APIX) and the Gambian Embassy in Dakar has staged the first episode of The Gambia-Senegal Economic, Trade, and Investment Forum at the Senegalese capital of Dakar with theme “Strengthening Sene-Gambia Brotherhood: From Formality to Simplicity”.
The Dakar conclave is attended by over 200 Gambians and Senegalese in government, private sector, businesses, and civil society organizations. The event provided the opportunity for 27 Gambian businesses to exhibit their products and network with their Senegalese counterparts by participating in; networking sessions, trade and investment meetings through; Business2Business, Business2Government, Government2Business, and Government2Government engagements.
The two-day event also witnessed a cultural and award night to deserving partners who supported the event between the two countries at the Radisson Blu Hotel climaxing what appears to be a rebirth of cordial relationship between the two brotherly countries.
The envisaged rebirth builds on existing political, economic, and social relations proposed by the Head of States of the two countries looking into critical investment areas such as Agriculture, Tourism, Energy, Manufacturing, Trade, Transport, Customs, Information & Digital Economy, Culture, Fisheries, Handicraft and Tailoring, among others.

IPAWAS Begins Banjul Technical Meeting
17 Sep 2024
News
A two-day technical meeting conveyed by the Investment Promotion Agencies of West African States (IPAWAS) is underway at a local hotel in Kololi.
The two-day meeting drew Investment experts from the 15 member states of the ECOWAS sub-region reviewing a statue that will define how investment promotion agencies will integrate and work together for the development of the region.
This technical conclave is a prelude to the CEOs annual general meeting where approvals are expected to be granted on strategic recommendations advanced by regional exports to promote investments, cross border trade and foreign direct investment facilitation amongst ECOWAS member states.
Delivering his welcoming remarks at the event, the CEO of the Gambia Investment and Export Promotion Agency welcomed delegates and expressed The Gambia’s peaceful democratic gains and strong political commitment that is changing the country’s business environment. He further expresses the country’s readiness to embracing new investment models anchored on integration to better serve the teeming aspiration of ECOWAS citizens.
The two-day event will exhaust all frameworks provided and examined by the regional association for consideration by the CEO’s meeting for approval. The Banjul meeting will further discuss plans on the proposed 2025 ECOWAS investment Forum and is expected to open more Foreign Direct Investment into the region.

Key Functions
Offering investor–facilitation services, the Agency acts as investors’ first point of contact, provides information on relevant procedures for setting up a business and helps form the necessary network of contacts in The Gambia for successful business operations.
GIEPA advises and collaborates with the Government of The Gambia, the private sector and civil society towards developing a globally competitive business environment. Key functions center on promoting the exportation of Gambian goods, developing and regulating The Gambia’s business park, formulating investment promotion, export and enterprise development strategies and policies geared towards the continuous improvement of the investment climate in The Gambia for private sector led growth.
Why Invest in The Gambia?
Business Friendly Environment
The Government of The Gambia is continually upgrading business procedures to transform The Gambia into a preferred location for profitable investments. Reforms to liberalize the ICT sector and the energy sector have already been completed. A variety of incentives for investors, including GIEPA's Special Investment Certificate (SIC) are also available.
Strategic Location & Access to West Africa
Driven by its geographical proximity to West African countries, The Gambia offers an excellent opportunity for investors to tap into a growing market with over 300m+ potential consumers. The Gambia also provides a port with access to shipping from the Atlantic ocean and a variety of preferential trade partners.
Liberal Market-Based Economy
Investors profit from a liberal, market-based economy characterized by sustained scale and monetary discipline, flexible exchange rate, and reliable laws.
The Gambia also ranked 41 out of 138 in the Labour Market Efficiency Index provided by the world Economic Forum in 2016-2017.
Source
Competitive Labor Costs
For decades, businesses have benefited from The Gambia’s abundant semi-skilled and unskilled labor force. Wages in The Gambia are substantially lower than in many West African countries across a variety of labor categories. At present, the minimum daily wage rate is $1.50 with average wages between $2.50 and $4 per day. This large pool of relatively cost-effective labor has helped businesses lower operational costs.
Peaceful and Politically-Stable Country
In 2018, The Gambia had the largest positive change in the Global Peace Index, moving up 35 places to rank 76th globally.
Source

How to Use Our Report Maker
Economic developers frequently need to generate reports for site selectors, interested businesses, private lenders and grant administrators. Golden Shovel has designed a way to generate these reports quickly and easily using the content, graphics, and resources already available on your website. Our Universal Report Maker tool is so easy that reports can be generated in meer minutes, rather than taking an entire afternoon.
The Universal Report Maker tool can be used to extract data from any page on your website, providing information on areas like workforce development, transportation, incentives, success stories, etc.
To use this convenient tool, do the following:
Step 1:
Visit the first page you want included in the report.
Step 2:
Click “Add to Report” – This is a white button with red text. If successful, an alert will show up saying that the page was “added to the report.”
Step 3:
Visit other pages you want added to the report and repeat step #2.
Step 4:
When on the last page, select the button for “View Custom Report.”
Step 5:
Click on the settings icon to customize the report and to create a cover page.
Step 6:
Select the print or PDF icon at the top to print or save the report.
Step 7:
Share in person or by email.
Congratulations! You’re done. Creating a report using Golden Shovel’s Universal Report Maker tool is that easy.

Key Functions
Offering investor–facilitation services, the Agency acts as investors’ first point of contact, provides information on relevant procedures for setting up a business and helps form the necessary network of contacts in The Gambia for successful business operations.
GIEPA advises and collaborates with the Government of The Gambia, the private sector and civil society towards developing a globally competitive business environment. Key functions center on promoting the exportation of Gambian goods, developing and regulating The Gambia’s business park, formulating investment promotion, export and enterprise development strategies and policies geared towards the continuous improvement of the investment climate in The Gambia for private sector led growth.
Why Invest in The Gambia?
Business Friendly Environment
The Government of The Gambia is continually upgrading business procedures to transform The Gambia into a preferred location for profitable investments. Reforms to liberalize the ICT sector and the energy sector have already been completed. A variety of incentives for investors, including GIEPA's Special Investment Certificate (SIC) are also available.
Strategic Location & Access to West Africa
Driven by its geographical proximity to West African countries, The Gambia offers an excellent opportunity for investors to tap into a growing market with over 300m+ potential consumers. The Gambia also provides a port with access to shipping from the Atlantic ocean and a variety of preferential trade partners.
Liberal Market-Based Economy
Investors profit from a liberal, market-based economy characterized by sustained scale and monetary discipline, flexible exchange rate, and reliable laws.
The Gambia also ranked 41 out of 138 in the Labour Market Efficiency Index provided by the world Economic Forum in 2016-2017.
Source
Competitive Labor Costs
For decades, businesses have benefited from The Gambia’s abundant semi-skilled and unskilled labor force. Wages in The Gambia are substantially lower than in many West African countries across a variety of labor categories. At present, the minimum daily wage rate is $1.50 with average wages between $2.50 and $4 per day. This large pool of relatively cost-effective labor has helped businesses lower operational costs.
Peaceful and Politically-Stable Country
In 2018, The Gambia had the largest positive change in the Global Peace Index, moving up 35 places to rank 76th globally.
Source

GIEPA Signs MOU with Ecosystem-Based Adaptation Project (EBA)
31 Aug 2023
News
We are thrilled to announce that the Gambia Investment and Export Promotion Agency (GIEPA) has officially signed a Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) with Large-Scale Ecosystem-Based Adaptation (EbA) Project earlier today. This landmark collaboration sets the stage for exciting opportunities and growth in the realm of entrepreneurship support, export development, and business advancement in The Gambia.
The MOU signing ceremony took place at EbA headquarters and was attended by prominent officials and representatives from both GIEPA and the EbA Project. This partnership aims to foster a conducive environment for local businesses and entrepreneurs, enhance investment prospects, and propel economic development in the region.
GIEPA, known for its commitment to promoting sustainable investment and export activities, sees this collaboration as a significant step towards achieving its goals. The EbA Project, renowned for its expertise in providing resources, training, and support for business growth, will work hand in hand with GIEPA to empower local businesses, facilitate market access, and encourage innovation. The EbA Project aims at developing a climate resilient, natural resource-based economy, funded by the Green Climate Fund (GCF) through a USD 20.5 million grant as well as USD 5 million from the Government of Gambia in co-financing. Natural resource-based production is a vital contributor to sustain the livelihoods of the Gambian population for both household consumption and trading.
Through joint initiatives, GIEPA and the EbA Project aspire to elevate The Gambia's business landscape, attract foreign investment, and enable homegrown enterprises to thrive on both local and international fronts.
This collaboration marks the beginning of a new chapter in The Gambia's economic journey, where public and private entities join forces to create a robust and sustainable business ecosystem.
"We are excited about the opportunities that this partnership will bring to our local entrepreneurs and investors. By combining GIEPA’ s knowledge of the local market with the EbA Project's expertise in natural resource-based production, we are confident in our ability to drive meaningful changes in the industry," said Mr. Ousainou Senghore, CEO of GIEPA.

Presidential Tour Highlights GIEPA-Facilitated Investment and Industrial Growth in Rural Gambia
19 Nov 2025
News
As part of his ongoing Meet the People Tour, His Excellency President Adama Barrow concluded his rural engagements with a visit to Gold Tree Company, a growing local manufacturer contributing to The Gambia’s industrialization and employment creation efforts.
Gold Tree Company currently produces plywood using locally sourced pongoon wood, employing approximately 30 Gambians. The company is a beneficiary of the Gambia Investment and Export Promotion Agency (GIEPA)’s Land for Investment Facility, which provided the land to support its operations; a key initiative aimed at unlocking land access for productive and transformative investments.
During the visit, GIEPA CEO Mr. Ousainou Senghore, alongside President Barrow and a delegation from the Chinese investment team, undertook a familiarization tour of the facility, gaining firsthand insight into the company’s operations, workforce, and expansion plans.
In a major development, Gold Tree is currently constructing a cashew and peanut processing plant, expected to be operational by 2026. Once completed, the project is anticipated to:
Strengthen local manufacturing capacity
Create new employment opportunities
Add value to domestic agricultural raw materials
Contribute to rural development, industrial growth, and export competitiveness
These outcomes align directly with GIEPA’s mandate to promote investment, support enterprise development, and enhance The Gambia’s export potential.
GIEPA remains committed to facilitating impactful investments that drive inclusive growth, private sector expansion, and economic transformation across The Gambia.

GiEPA SIGNS MoU WITH ITS SENEGALESE COUNTERPART APIX
26 Oct 2023
News
By virtue of the legal mandate for the two respective Investment Promotion Agencies (IPA), the signed Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) between the Gambia Investment and Export Promotion Agency (GiEPA) and the Agence Nationale Chargee de la Promotion des Investissements et des Grands Travaux Au Senegal (APIX), has become the lead document in the highly charged two days Senegal-Gambia Economic, Trade, and Investment Forum held at Radisson Blu Hotel in Dakar-Senegal.
The carefully crafted and vetted twelve (12) articled MoU is anchored on the signed joint communique of the bilateral agreement on Senegalo-Gambian Roadmap Three (3), between the Gambia and Senegal during the last Presidential Council Meeting held in Banjul in August 2023.
The document signed by GiEPA CEO - Ousainou Senghore and APIX DG- Abdoulaye Balde entails mutual cooperation as lead agencies further assigned to facilitating the execution of government policy and private sector participation in the economic development of the respective countries.
The new protocol seeks to facilitate, strengthen, and promote cooperation between the two countries, provide necessary support to business investments in the short, medium, and long term as well as enable joint industrial processes, training, financing, licensing, and access to finance. The agreement further entails offering the much-needed support to the Senegalo-Gambian Permanent Secretariat and respective government ministries between the two countries in Agriculture, Tourism Energy, Manufacturing, Trade, Logistics, Transport, Customs, Information and Digital Economy, Culture, Fisheries etc.
The signing Ceremony was presided over by the Vice President of The Republic of The Gambia - His Excellency Muhammad B.S Jallow and The Prime Minister of Senegal - His Excellency Amadou Ba. The much-awaited formality of the Economic, Trade, and Investment forum climaxed with the exchange of gifts by the respective heads of IPAs and the briefing of their Vice presidents for government support to implement the MoU.

Key Functions
Offering investor–facilitation services, the Agency acts as investors’ first point of contact, provides information on relevant procedures for setting up a business and helps form the necessary network of contacts in The Gambia for successful business operations.
GIEPA advises and collaborates with the Government of The Gambia, the private sector and civil society towards developing a globally competitive business environment. Key functions center on promoting the exportation of Gambian goods, developing and regulating The Gambia’s business park, formulating investment promotion, export and enterprise development strategies and policies geared towards the continuous improvement of the investment climate in The Gambia for private sector led growth.
Why Invest in The Gambia?
Business Friendly Environment
The Government of The Gambia is continually upgrading business procedures to transform The Gambia into a preferred location for profitable investments. Reforms to liberalize the ICT sector and the energy sector have already been completed. A variety of incentives for investors, including GIEPA's Special Investment Certificate (SIC) are also available.
Strategic Location & Access to West Africa
Driven by its geographical proximity to West African countries, The Gambia offers an excellent opportunity for investors to tap into a growing market with over 300m+ potential consumers. The Gambia also provides a port with access to shipping from the Atlantic ocean and a variety of preferential trade partners.
Liberal Market-Based Economy
Investors profit from a liberal, market-based economy characterized by sustained scale and monetary discipline, flexible exchange rate, and reliable laws.
The Gambia also ranked 41 out of 138 in the Labour Market Efficiency Index provided by the world Economic Forum in 2016-2017.
Source
Competitive Labor Costs
For decades, businesses have benefited from The Gambia’s abundant semi-skilled and unskilled labor force. Wages in The Gambia are substantially lower than in many West African countries across a variety of labor categories. At present, the minimum daily wage rate is $1.50 with average wages between $2.50 and $4 per day. This large pool of relatively cost-effective labor has helped businesses lower operational costs.
Peaceful and Politically-Stable Country
In 2018, The Gambia had the largest positive change in the Global Peace Index, moving up 35 places to rank 76th globally.

The 7th Edition of the Financial Afrik Awards
24 Jan 2026
News
The 7th Edition of the Financial Afrik Awards
The Gambia Investment & Export Promotion Agency (GIEPA), in partnership with The Cornerstones Group and Financial Afrik Awards, successfully hosted the 7th Edition of the Financial Afrik Awards 2026 at the Sir Dawda Kairaba Jawara International Conference Center. The two-day event, held on January 22nd and 23rd, 2026, centered around the theme:
“Public-Private Partnership as an Alternative to Public Debt.”
Highlights of the Event
The gathering brought together investors from across Africa and the Arab world, making it a true Arab-Africa Round table.
Breakout sessions facilitated one-on-one meetings, B2B engagements, and networking opportunities.
The celebration of African excellence was marked by the presentation of awards to outstanding African businesses.
Distinguished Attendance
The President of the Republic of The Gambia, H.E. Adama Barrow, attended with a high-level delegation. In his keynote address, he expressed pride that The Gambia was chosen as the host nation for this edition, describing it as an honor. He reaffirmed his government’s commitment to supporting initiatives that attract investment and foster economic growth in the country. Under his leadership, with infrastructure designated as a priority sector, the government stands ready to support initiatives that bring investors into direct engagement with their Gambian stakeholders around the same table
Key Remarks
Mr. Ousainou Senghore
, CEO of GIEPA, delivered the welcoming remarks. He emphasized The Gambia’s reputation as a safe haven for investors and assured that investments in the country are well protected.
The
“Invest in Gambia”
session, chaired by
Mr. Hamey B. Jawara
, Senior Manager of Investment Promotion and Facilitation, showcased priority investment projects under the Public-Private Partnership framework.
Engagements and Closing
President Barrow toured the exhibition booths, engaging directly with institutions and businesses to gain firsthand insight into their operations. The event concluded with a group photo, followed by a Gala Dinner and awards ceremony, marking the official close of the 7th Edition of the Financial Afrik Awards 2026.

Top 5 Reasons to Invest in The Gambia
Peaceful and Politically-Stable Country
The Gambia is recognised as one of the most politically stable countries in West Africa. In 2018, the nation made the largest positive improvement on the Global Peace Index, climbing 35 places to rank 76th globally. By 2023, The Gambia had further improved its standing, continuing to uphold its reputation as a peaceful and secure destination for investment and tourism.
Source:
https://countryeconomy.com/demography/global-peace-index/gambia
Competitive Labor Costs
For decades, businesses in The Gambia have benefited from an abundant workforce of semi-skilled and unskilled labour. Wages in The Gambia remain significantly lower than in many West African countries across various labour categories, providing a cost advantage for businesses. As of 2024, the minimum daily wage is
$0.92 USD
, with average daily wages ranging between
$2.50 and $4.00 USD
. This cost-effective labour pool enables businesses to operate efficiently while maintaining competitive operational costs.
Source:
https://www.rivermate.com/guides/gambia/salary
Liberal Market Economy
The Gambia maintains a liberal market economy characterised by sustained growth, monetary discipline, a flexible exchange rate system, and robust legal frameworks. These elements provide investors with a predictable and business-friendly environment.
Economic Freedom
: In the
2023 Index of Economic Freedom
, The Gambia achieved a score of
57.9
, ranking
101st
globally and
11th
among 47 countries in the Sub-Saharan Africa region. This score reflects the country's ongoing efforts to enhance economic freedom and foster a conducive environment for business operations.
Labor Market Efficiency
: The Gambia has demonstrated a commitment to improving labour market efficiency. The
2022–2023 Gambia Labor Force Survey
provides comprehensive data on employment trends, informing policies aimed at enhancing workforce participation and productivity.
These developments underscore The Gambia's dedication to creating a dynamic and open economy, appealing to both domestic and international investors.
Sources:
https://indexdotnet.azurewebsites.net/index/pdf/2023/countries/2023_IndexofEconomicFreedom-The-Gambia.pdf
https://www.gbosdata.org/downloads-file/480-2022-23-gambia-labour-force-survey-report-glfs
Strategic Location & Access to West Africa
Strategically located on the Atlantic Ocean and surrounded by Senegal, The Gambia serves as an ideal gateway to the wider West African market, which encompasses over
300 million consumers
. The Port of Banjul provides access to key shipping routes, facilitating trade and serving as a regional logistics hub. In addition, The Gambia benefits from preferential trade agreements with major global markets, including the
European Union
, the
United States
(under AGOA), and ECOWAS, offering businesses significant export advantages.
Source:
https://www.trade.gov/country-commercial-guides/gambia-market-opportunities
Business Friendly Environment
The Gambian Government continues to implement reforms to enhance its business environment, making it a preferred destination for profitable investments. Recent measures include the liberalisation of the
ICT
and
energy sectors
, improvements in regulatory processes, and streamlined procedures for setting up businesses. The
Gambia Investment and Export Promotion Agency (GIEPA)
offers a range of incentives to investors, including the
Special Investment Certificate (SIC)
, which provides tax holidays and other benefits for eligible businesses. These initiatives reflect the government’s commitment to fostering a transparent and supportive environment for investors.
Sources:
https://www.giepa.gm/industries
https://www.trade.gov/country-commercial-guides/gambia-market-opportunities

GiEPA and Regenerative Hub Embarks on Scoping Mission For +5 Thousand Hectares of Planned Agricultural Investment in Cereal Production.
12 May 2025
News
The two-day land scoping mission, project sensitization and consultation meetings organized by the Gambia Investment and Export Promotion Agency (GiEPA) and prospective investors mobilized by Regenerative Hub covered Upper River Region (URR) South from 12
th
to 13
th
May 2025. The initiative anchored on the national land for investment incentive initiative took both investors, facilitators and partners to Kulari and Kundam Villages respectively in the URR.
The proposal advanced as per a Regenerative Hub mobilization seeks to transform degraded land into productive, and climate-resilient agricultural ecosystems across Africa. Their current flagship pilot in The Gambia spans 1,750 hectares, combining regenerative agriculture, agroforestry, and carbon capture to create profitable, sustainable business models that are resilient and climate smart.
A new proposed investments in cereal production by the Sadot Group once finalized will enable cultivation of 10 thousand hectares of agricultural land that will drive food-self-sufficiency and boost export development, employment creation and technology transfer to communities, agronomist and experts.
By integrating innovative farming with community development, Regenerative Hub empowers local farmers especially women and youth while generating long-term returns for investors facilitated by GiEPA. The project is designed as a scalable model for expansion across Africa’s Great Green Wall, offering a unique intersection of environmental restoration and economic opportunity.
The delegation comprised of GiEPA CEO Ousainou Senghore, Regenerative Hub Cofounder – Jaha Dukureh, Mr Kailesh Ashani from Coo-Africa, Mr Igor Yasnogorodsky Eco soil President of The Gambia, Mr Sam Friedman – Sadot Group Special adviser, and GiEPA's Omar Jammeh.

GIEPA and the Entrepreneurship and Private Sector Development Project (EPSDP) Spark Innovation and Growth: Successful Conclusion of MSME Pitching Competition in ICT & Agriculture Value Chains
30 Aug 2023
News
The Gambia Investment and Export Promotion Agency (GIEPA), through the EPSDP, has marked another milestone in fostering innovation and growth within the country's Micro, Small, and Medium Enterprises (MSMEs) sector.
The EPSD Project's commitment to promoting sustainable economic development and facilitating a conducive environment for businesses was evident throughout the competition. Entrepreneurs representing the ICT and Agriculture value chains industries showcased their groundbreaking ideas, products, and services to a panel of esteemed judges comprising industry experts, investors, and business leaders.
The recently concluded MSME Pitching Competition, held with the aim of discovering and nurturing local entrepreneurial talent, witnessed a remarkable display of creativity, resilience, and ingenuity within the ICT and Agricultural value chains.
The competition's primary objective was to provide MSMEs with a platform to pitch their business concepts and secure potential investments, partnerships, and valuable mentorship opportunities. Through the EPSD Project, the agency has been steadfast in its mission to equip local entrepreneurs with the necessary skills, resources, and connections to thrive in today's competitive market landscape.
The MSME Pitching Competition serves as a testament to GIEPA’ s dedication to empowering small businesses in The Gambia. We are immensely proud of the participants who demonstrated exceptional innovation and determination, embodying the spirit of entrepreneurship that is vital for our nation's economic progress.
As a result of the MSME Pitching Competition, two outstanding businesses emerged as winners, gaining recognition for their inventive ideas and viable business models. The winners will receive $8000 worth of equipment.
GIEPA remains committed to nurturing an ecosystem where MSMEs can flourish and contribute significantly to The Gambia's economic growth and job creation. By fostering innovation and entrepreneurial spirit, the agency continues to play a pivotal role in driving the nation's economic transformation.

UK Trade Mission to The Gambia – 2nd – 3rd February 2026
2 Feb 2026
News
The UK
Trade Mission to The Gambia was
held on
2nd and 3rd February 2026
at the prestigious Sir Dawda Kairaba Jawara International Conference Center
in Bijilo. The first-ever UK Trade Mission
brought together high‑level government officials, UK businesses, and Gambian private‑sector leaders for two days of strategic engagement, collaboration, and investment exploration.
The event was
graced by the Vice President of The Republic of The Gambia, His Excellency
, Muhammed B.S Jallow accompanied by ministers, senior policymakers, and dignitaries from key national institutions. Supported by
GIEPA
, the
Ministry of Trade
, Industry Regional Integration and Employment,
Ministry of Foreign Affairs
,
Ministry of Tourism, Arts and Culture
,
Ministry of Agriculture
,
Ministry of Petroleum and Energy and Mines
, and the
Ministry of Health
, the mission successfully created a platform for impactful discussions and long‑term partnership opportunities.
A Strategic Gateway for UK–Gambia Collaboration
As West Africa’s smallest yet vibrant economic hub, The Gambia offers promising opportunities for foreign investment, and the two‑day convergence provided UK businesses with critical insights and access to four economic priority sectors:
Agriculture
– agribusiness, processing, and export expansion
Tourism
– hospitality development and sustainable tourism ventures
Health
– medical infrastructure, pharmaceuticals, and digital health
Energy
– renewable solutions, power generation, and efficiency projects
The Gambia shared with delegates the country’s evolving regulatory systems, investment incentives, and sector‑specific growth areas through presentations and panel discussions by distinguished personalities from both public and private sectors.
Program Highlights
High‑Level Government Engagement
Throughout the sessions at the International Conference Center, UK delegates met with the
Vice President of the Republic of The Gambia, the Ministers of Trade Industry Regional Integration and Employment and Agriculture and senior officials of the
Ministry of
Foreign Affairs and International Cooperation.
Ministry of Tourism Arts and Culture, Ministry of Finance and Economic Affairs and the CEO and staff of the Gambia Investment and Export Promotion Agency.
These meetings elaborated on investment pathways, regulatory frameworks, and government priorities for sustainable development.
Business Networking & B2B Engagements
The mission featured:
One‑on‑one meetings between UK companies and Gambian private‑sector leaders
Networking dinners and cocktail receptions organized by the British High Commissioner to The Gambia, H.E Harriet King at her Residence.
Panel discussions on the local business climate and market-entry strategies
On‑Site Sector Visits
Delegates were given the opportunity to visit tourist attraction sites such as the famous Monkey Park and Kachikally Museum and Crocodile Pool in Bijilo and Bakau respectively.
These immersive visits showcased real-world opportunities and partnership potential in the country.
Cultural Immersion
Participants also enjoyed The Gambia’s renowned hospitality, vibrant culture, and local attractions — reinforcing people-to-people connections essential for long‑term cooperation.
Key Outcomes
The successful trade mission resulted in:
New strategic partnerships and investment interest across key sectors
Greater recognition of
The Gambia as a competitive destination for UK investment
Practical roadmaps for UK companies seeking market entry
Strengthened diplomatic and economic ties between the UK and The Gambia
A New Chapter for Bilateral Economic Cooperation
The Trade Mission stands as a major milestone in advancing mutual economic growth between the two nations. With strong government support and meaningful private‑sector engagement, the mission has set the stage for innovative partnerships, impactful investments, and long-term collaboration that will contribute to The Gambia’s sustainable development journey.

GiEPA Supports 28 Businesses to Exhibit at the 18th Edition of GCCI Trade Exhibition
13 May 2025
News
The Gambia Investment and Export Promotion Agency (GiEPA) through its development partner The Gambia Inclusive and Resilient Agricultural Value Chain Development Project (GIRAV) have supported 28 SMEs to exhibit at the GCCI trade exhibition.
The strategic provision is anchored on the 2023-2027 GiEPA Strategy Plan Goal 2 “Promote and develop the export potential of businesses in The Gambia”. This seeks to
strengthen the export capability of SMEs and further provide market access whilst supporting critical private sector players like
Chambers of Commerce, Business Service Organizations and Associations.
As part of this development, the President of the Republic of The Gambia His Excellency Mr Adama Barrow visited the GiEPA Pavillion housing the 28 supported businesses. This move anchored on the GiEPA’s Strategy Plan of the President as the number one promoter of Made in The Gambia Products enabled the Gambian leader to visit the various stalls to see exclusively Made in the Gambia Products, interact with the entrepreneurs, patronize their products, and acknowledge their contributions to national development.
It is believed that such market access support initiatives will prepare Gambian SMEs to partner and explore linkage opportunities to reach more market
preferences such as ECOWAS-ETLS, AfCFTA, AGOA, ACO-EU and EBA.
Reacting to the support provided by GiEPA and its partner GIRAV, entrepreneurs expressed their gratitude to the government for the policy environment and called for more coordinated interventions from development partners through GiEPA. This is to ensure tracking and coaching to ensure export readiness leveraging on subregional and regional treaties and ensuring continuity.

One-Stop Shop Entrepreneurship and Gender-Base Violence Support Center Launched
12 Sep 2024
News
The center is built by The International Trade Centre (ITC) and the United Nations Fund for Population Affairs (UNFPA) under the United Nations Trust Fund for Human Security and the United Nation Migration Multi-Partner Trust Fund projects. The initiative seeks to address economic and social challenges, supporting entrepreneurship, cross border trade and survivors of gender-based violence.
The center will provide services such as business advisory, decentralized business registration, packaging and labelling assistance and market linages. These services aim to enhance the competitiveness of micro, small and medium -sized enterprises, subsequently promoting innovation and creating employment opportunities particularly at the regional level.
The newly inaugurated Centre by the Permanent Secretary at the Trade Ministry on behalf of the Minister of Trade, Industry, Regional Integration and Employment – Hon. Baboucarr Joof, will address challenges faced by small-scale cross-border traders in completing border formalities by providing the necessary information and existing resources.
In addition, the GBV support center will provide critical health, legal and psycho-social services to survivors in a safe space with a view to promoting prevention as well as justice and healing.
The 7 million Dalasis edifice includes a training room, shared offices for Business Support Organization (BSOs), consultation rooms for GBV survivors, a meeting room and an innovation hub. These facilities now handed over to the Gambia Investment and Export Promotion Agency (GiEPA) will facilitate services around Farafenni and surrounding communities.

GIEPA Champions Gambian Enterprises at World Halal Summit 2025 in Istanbul
25 Nov 2025
News
Theme: “Strengthening the Halal Industry via Innovation & Excellence”
The Gambia Investment and Export Promotion Agency (GIEPA) proudly leads a delegation of five Gambian enterprises at the 11th edition of the World Halal Summit & Exhibition 2025, currently taking place in Istanbul, Türkiye. As part of its core mandate to promote trade, attract investment, and support the internationalization of Gambian businesses, GIEPA’s participation underscores The Gambia’s growing commitment to the global halal economy.
Recognized as the world’s largest and most prestigious gathering of halal industry stakeholders, the summit; organized by Discover Events in collaboration with the Standards and Metrology Institute for Islamic Countries (SMIIC) serves as a strategic platform for halal trade, policy dialogue, innovation, and cross-border collaboration. This year’s theme,
“Strengthening the Halal Industry via Innovation & Excellence,”
resonates strongly with GIEPA’s vision of fostering competitive, export-ready enterprises that meet international standards.
Elevating Gambian Brands on the Global Stage
Through its support for five dynamic Gambian businesses, GIEPA is facilitating access to new markets, connecting local entrepreneurs with global buyers, and showcasing the country’s potential in halal-certified goods and services. The delegation spans sectors such as agro-processing, cosmetics, food and beverage, and ethical lifestyle products; each aligned with the values of sustainability, quality, and faith-based compliance.
This initiative reflects GIEPA’s strategic focus on enhancing The Gambia’s export base, promoting value addition, and positioning the country as a credible player in the halal value chain. By participating in high-impact global platforms like the World Halal Summit, GIEPA continues to create pathways for Gambian SMEs to scale, innovate, and thrive in competitive international markets.
A Global Convergence of Halal Excellence
The summit’s comprehensive agenda covers a wide range of topics critical to the future of the halal industry, including:
Halal food innovation and certification
Quality infrastructure and global standards
Supply chain integrity and traceability
Islamic finance and ethical investment
Halal pharmaceuticals and healthcare
Tourism, hospitality, and modest fashion
Consumer behavior and market trends
Scientific research and service excellence
With over 50,000 visitors, 9,000 delegates, 500+ exhibitors, and 100+ participating countries, the World Halal Summit 2025 offers an unparalleled opportunity for Gambian enterprises to engage with global partners, explore export opportunities, and contribute to shaping the future of the halal economy.
Advancing GIEPA’s Mission Through Global Engagement
GIEPA’s presence at the summit is a testament to its ongoing efforts to promote The Gambia as a competitive destination for investment and trade. By empowering local businesses to participate in international forums, GIEPA is not only enhancing the visibility of Gambian products but also reinforcing the country’s commitment to sustainable development, innovation, and inclusive economic growth.
As the halal economy continues to expand, GIEPA remains dedicated to ensuring that Gambian enterprises are equipped, recognized, and ready to seize emerging opportunities on the global stage.
GIEPA’s management and staff would like to thank the Turkish Ambassador and team to the Gambia for his valuable support.

GiEPA & PARTNERS OBSERVE CULTURAL NIGHT IN DAKAR
27 Oct 2023
News
The climax of the two-days Economic, Trade and Investment Forum was marked with a gala dinner rooted on strengthening Senegambian arts and culture. The event has its main guest being the Minister for Tourism and Culture Hon. Hamat Bah and his Senegalese counterpart - Aliou Sow.
The gallery marked with display of art and culture by lead performing artists from Senegal - Titi and The Gambia’s Jaliba Kuyateh marks an embodiment of culture and heritage between the two countries. In a raft of crafted speeches by the two culture ministers including the Gambia’s Foreign Affairs Minister - Dr Momodou Tangara, relations between the two countries are symbiotic but bonded with bloodlines spanning for centuries. The urge by Gambia’s firebrand politician and tourism minister - Hon Hamat Bah has been for the two countries to strengthen relations as advanced by the two heads of state, and improve trade and investment opportunities without let or hindrance.
The ceremony capping the first historic partnership between The Gambia Investment and Export Promotion Agency (GiEPA), the Agence Nationale chargee de la Promotion des Investissements et des Grands Travaux au Senegal (APIX) accorded recognition to deserving companies that supported the exercise. Key Businesses that supported the events are predominantly Special Investment Certificate holders including Bell, GACH Global, SRN Steel, Nassim Trading. Others include Transvie - a senegalese international health company who are currently working on transforming health coverage for Gambians.

Key Functions
Offering investor–facilitation services, the Agency acts as investors’ first point of contact, provides information on relevant procedures for setting up a business and helps form the necessary network of contacts in The Gambia for successful business operations.
GIEPA advises and collaborates with the Government of The Gambia, the private sector and civil society towards developing a globally competitive business environment. Key functions center on promoting the exportation of Gambian goods, developing and regulating The Gambia’s business park, formulating investment promotion, export and enterprise development strategies and policies geared towards the continuous improvement of the investment climate in The Gambia for private sector led growth.
Why Invest in The Gambia?
Business Friendly Environment
The Government of The Gambia is continually upgrading business procedures to transform The Gambia into a preferred location for profitable investments. Reforms to liberalize the ICT sector and the energy sector have already been completed. A variety of incentives for investors, including GIEPA's Special Investment Certificate (SIC) are also available.
Strategic Location & Access to West Africa
Driven by its geographical proximity to West African countries, The Gambia offers an excellent opportunity for investors to tap into a growing market with over 300m+ potential consumers. The Gambia also provides a port with access to shipping from the Atlantic ocean and a variety of preferential trade partners.
Liberal Market-Based Economy
Investors profit from a liberal, market-based economy characterized by sustained scale and monetary discipline, flexible exchange rate, and reliable laws.
The Gambia also ranked 41 out of 138 in the Labour Market Efficiency Index provided by the world Economic Forum in 2016-2017.
Source
Competitive Labor Costs
For decades, businesses have benefited from The Gambia’s abundant semi-skilled and unskilled labor force. Wages in The Gambia are substantially lower than in many West African countries across a variety of labor categories. At present, the minimum daily wage rate is $1.50 with average wages between $2.50 and $4 per day. This large pool of relatively cost-effective labor has helped businesses lower operational costs.
Peaceful and Politically-Stable Country
In 2018, The Gambia had the largest positive change in the Global Peace Index, moving up 35 places to rank 76th globally.
Source

Key Functions
Offering investor–facilitation services, the Agency acts as investors’ first point of contact, provides information on relevant procedures for setting up a business and helps form the necessary network of contacts in The Gambia for successful business operations.
GIEPA advises and collaborates with the Government of The Gambia, the private sector and civil society towards developing a globally competitive business environment. Key functions center on promoting the exportation of Gambian goods, developing and regulating The Gambia’s business park, formulating investment promotion, export and enterprise development strategies and policies geared towards the continuous improvement of the investment climate in The Gambia for private sector led growth.
Why Invest in The Gambia?
Business Friendly Environment
The Government of The Gambia is continually upgrading business procedures to transform The Gambia into a preferred location for profitable investments. Reforms to liberalize the ICT sector and the energy sector have already been completed. A variety of incentives for investors, including GIEPA's Special Investment Certificate (SIC) are also available.
Strategic Location & Access to West Africa
Driven by its geographical proximity to West African countries, The Gambia offers an excellent opportunity for investors to tap into a growing market with over 300m+ potential consumers. The Gambia also provides a port with access to shipping from the Atlantic ocean and a variety of preferential trade partners.
Liberal Market-Based Economy
Investors profit from a liberal, market-based economy characterized by sustained scale and monetary discipline, flexible exchange rate, and reliable laws.
The Gambia also ranked 41 out of 138 in the Labour Market Efficiency Index provided by the world Economic Forum in 2016-2017.
Source
Competitive Labor Costs
For decades, businesses have benefited from The Gambia’s abundant semi-skilled and unskilled labor force. Wages in The Gambia are substantially lower than in many West African countries across a variety of labor categories. At present, the minimum daily wage rate is $1.50 with average wages between $2.50 and $4 per day. This large pool of relatively cost-effective labor has helped businesses lower operational costs.
Peaceful and Politically-Stable Country
In 2018, The Gambia had the largest positive change in the Global Peace Index, moving up 35 places to rank 76th globally.
Source

The Gambia-Pakistan Trade and Investment Forum
29 May 2025
News
A delegation of Eight Pakistani Business leaders and investors visited the Smiling Coast as part of an Economic, Trade, and Investment Forum held at Kombo Beach Hotel on 28th May 2025. The event explored mutually beneficial opportunities in all the key sectors such as agriculture, textile, housing and real estate development, manufacturing, renewable energy, information technology construction, infrastructure, vehicle exports and trade services.
The day's engagement was organized by the Gambia Investment and Export Promotion Agency (GiEPA) in collaboration with its parent ministry, the Ministry of Trade, Industry, Regional Integration and Employment and the Pakistani Embassy in Dakar through their Gambian Chancery in Banjul.
The forum is part of the Agency's quest to facilitating FDIs for improved macro-economic stability through job creation and boosting industrialization which remains a consonant in the GiEPA 2023 – 2027 Strategy Plan. Key discussed areas under this plan focused on promoting domestic investments in critical areas like light Manufacturing for clothing & textile, food & beverages, metal & metallic works, wood & leather, processing, and chemicals used in soap and plastic making.
The forum also facilitated collaboration amongst both Gambian and Pakistani businesses creating greater opportunities for growth, development and partnerships.
The event witnessed several presentations done by GiEPA CEO - Ousainou Senghore as well as presentations made by all the visiting Pakistani businesses who were later facilitated to held B2B with their Gambian counterparts and B2G meeting.
